TEACHING STRATEGIES AND DECISIONS

To cement understanding, it is important to have students interact meaningfully with new words. Consider using the new words in any number of the following exercises:

  • Write example and nonexample sentences that use the new words.
  • Answer hypothetical situations that use the new words.
  • Craft analogies.
  • Connect the meaning of the words to texts or topics they have previously studied.
  • Have students reword sentences to use the new terms.
  • Respond to true and false statements that use the words.
  • Use the words in a discussion.
  • Complete a Word Map for one of the words.
  • Complete a Vocabulary in Context Tool for one of the words.
  • Complete an Open Sort or Closed Sort for categories of words.
  • Draw a visual representation of the word.
  • Explore and explain how two words are related conceptually to one another.

STUDENT SUPPORT AND DIFFERENTIATION

As students learn more vocabulary, it is important to provide opportunities to use the words in meaningful contexts, such as those activities mentioned above. These opportunities provide students, particularly English learners, a tremendous chance to increase their cognitive academic language proficiency (CALP). Students, particularly English learners, often employ basic interpersonal communicative skills (BICS). You might combine new vocabulary with sentence starters, such as those found in the Academic Discussion Reference Guide, to provide opportunities for English learners to practice using academic language in the classroom.
